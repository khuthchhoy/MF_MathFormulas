Compound Interest Formulas
Future value: $A$
Initial deposit: $C$
Annual rate of interest: $r$
Number of years invested: $t$
Number of times compounded per year: $n$

  1. General Compound Interest Formula: $$A = C\left(1+\dfrac{r}{n}\right)^{nt}$$
  2. Simplified Compound Interest Formula:
    If interest is compounded once per year, then the previous formula simplifies to: $$A = C(1+r)^{t}$$
  3. Continuous Compound Interest:
    If interest is compounded continually ($n \rightarrow \infty$), then: $$A = Ce^{rt}$$
