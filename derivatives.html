Definition of a Derivative

Suppose $y=f(x)$. The derivative of $y$ or $f(x)$ is defined as $$\dfrac{dy}{dx} = \lim_{h\to 0}\dfrac{f(x+h) - f(x)}{h} = \lim_{\Delta x \to 0}\dfrac{f(x+\Delta x) - f(x)}{\Delta x}$$ where $h=\Delta x$. The derivative is also denoted by $y'$, $\dfrac{dy}{dx}$,or $f'(x)$. The process of taking a derivative is called differentiation.

General Rules of Differentiation

In the following, $u,v,w$ are function of $x$; $a,b,c,n$ are constants (restricted if indicated); $e=2.71828...$ is the natural base of logarithms; $\ln u$ in the natural logarithm of $u$ where it is assumed that $u\gt 0$ and all angles are in radians.

$$\begin{aligned} & \frac{d}{dx}(c) = 0 \\\\ & \frac{d}{dx}(cx) = c \\\\ & \frac{d}{dx}\left(cx^n \right) = ncx^{n-1} \\\\ & \frac{d}{dx}(u \pm v \pm w\pm \cdots) = \frac{du}{dx} \pm \frac{dv}{dx} \pm \frac{dw}{dx} \pm \cdots \\\\ & \frac{d}{dx}(cu) = c\frac{du}{dx} \\\\ & \frac{d}{dx}(uv) = u\frac{dv}{dx} + v\frac{du}{dx} \\\\ & \frac{d}{dx}(uvw) = uv\frac{dw}{dx} + uw\frac{dv}{dx} + vw\frac{du}{dx} \\\\ & \frac{d}{dx}\left( \frac{u}{v} \right) = \frac{v(du/dx) - u(dv/dx)}{v^2} \\\\ & \frac{d}{dx}\left( u^n \right) = nu^{n-1}\frac{du}{dx} \\\\ & \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{dy}{du}\cdot\frac{du}{dx},\quad\text{(Chain rule)} \\\\ & \frac{du}{dx} = \frac{1}{dx/du}\\\\ & \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{dy/du}{dx/du} \end{aligned}$$
