Alternating Series

  1. The Alternating Series Test (Leibniz's Theorem):
    Let $\{a_{n}\}$ be a sequence of positive numbers such that $a_{n+1} \lt a_{n}$ for all $n$ and $\lim_{n\rightarrow\infty} a_{n} = 0$.
    Then the alternating series $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} (-1)^{n} a_{n}$ and $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} (-1)^{n-1} a_{n}$ both converge.
  2. Absolute Convergence
    A series $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} a_{n}$ is absolutely convergent if the series $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} |a_{n}|$ is convergent.
    If the series $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}a_{n}$ is absolutely convergent then it is convergent.
  3. Conditional Convergence
    A series $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}a_{n}$ is conditionally convergent if the series is convergent but is not absolutely convergent.

Power Series

Real numbers: $x, x_{0}$
Whole number: $n$
Radius of Convergence: $R$

  1. Power Series in $x$: $$\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}a_{n}x^{n}=a_{0}+a_{1}x+a_{2}x^{2}+\dots+a_{n}x^{n}+\dots$$
  2. Power Series in $(x-x_{0})$: $$\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}a_{n}(x-x_{0})^{n}=a_{0}+a_{1}(x-x_{0})+a_{2}(x-x_{0})^{2}+\dots+a_{n}(x-x_{0})^{n}+\dots$$
  3. Interval of Convergence:
    The set of those values of $x$ for which the function $f(x)=\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}a_{n}(x-x_{0})^{n}$ is convergent is called the interval of convergence.
  4. Radius of Convergence
    If the interval of convergence is $(x_{0}-R, x_{0}+R)$ for some $R \ge 0$, then $R$ is called the radius of convergence. It is given as: $$R = \lim_{n \to \infty} \dfrac{1}{\sqrt[n]{|a_{n}|}} \text{ or } R = \lim_{n \to \infty} \left| \dfrac{a_{n}}{a_{n+1}} \right|$$

Differentiation and Integration of Power Series

Continuous function: $f(x)$
Power series: $\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} a_{n}x^{n}$
Whole number: $n$
Radius of Convergence: $R$

  1. Differentiation of Power Series
    Let $f(x) = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} a_{n}x^{n} = a_{0} + a_{1}x + a_{2}x^{2} + \dots$ for $|x| \gt R$.
    Then, for $|x| \lt R$, $f(x)$ is continuous, the derivative $f^{\prime}(x)$ exists and: $$f^{\prime}(x) = \dfrac{d}{dx} a_{0} + \dfrac{d}{dx} a_{1}x + \dfrac{d}{dx} a_{2}x^{2} + \dots $$ $$= a_{1} + 2a_{2}x + 3a_{3}x^{2} + \dots = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} na_{n}x^{n-1} $$
  2. Integration of Power Series
    Let $f(x)=\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}a_{n}x^{n}=a_{0}+a_{1}x+a_{2}x^{2}+\dots$ for $|x|\lt R$.
    Then, for $|x|\lt R$ the indefinite integral $\int f(x)dx$ exists: $$\int f(x)dx=\int a_{0}dx+\int a_{1}xdx+\int a_{2}x^{2}dx+\dots$$ $$=a_{0}x+a_{1}\frac{x^{2}}{2}+a_{2}\frac{x^{3}}{3}+\dots=\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}a_{n}\frac{x^{n+1}}{n+1}+C $$

Taylor and Maclaurin Series

Whole number: $n$
Differentiable function: $f(x)$
Remainder term: $R_{n}$

  1. Taylor Series: $$f(x)=\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}f^{(n)}(a)\frac{(x-a)^{n}}{n!}=f(a)+f^{\prime}(a)(x-a)+\frac{f^{\prime\prime}(a)(x-a)^{2}}{2!}+\dots+\frac{f^{(n)}(a)(x-a)^{n}}{n!}+R_{n}$$
  2. The Remainder After $n+1$ Terms is given by: $$R_{n}=\frac{f^{(n+1)}(\xi)(x-a)^{n+1}}{(n+1)!}, a\lt \xi\lt x $$
  3. Maclaurin Series: $$f(x)=\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}f^{(n)}(0)\frac{x^{n}}{n!}=f(0)+f^{\prime}(0)x+\frac{f^{\prime\prime}(0)x^{2}}{2!}+\dots+\frac{f^{(n)}(0)x^{n}}{n!}+R_{n}$$

Power Series Expansions for Some Functions

Whole number: $n$
Real number: $x$

  1. Exponential function: $$e^{x}=1+x+\dfrac{x^{2}}{2!}+\dfrac{x^{3}}{3!}+\dots+\dfrac{x^{n}}{n!}+\dots $$
  2. General exponential function: $$a^{x}=1+\dfrac{x \ln a}{1!}+\dfrac{(x \ln a)^{2}}{2!}+\dfrac{(x \ln a)^{3}}{3!}+\dots+\dfrac{(x \ln a)^{n}}{n!}+\dots $$
  3. Natural logarithm of $(1+x)$ (for $-1 \lt x \le 1$): $$\ln(1+x)=x-\dfrac{x^{2}}{2}+\dfrac{x^{3}}{3}-\dfrac{x^{4}}{4}+\dots+\dfrac{(-1)^{n}x^{n+1}}{n+1}\pm\dots $$
  4. Logarithm of a quotient (for $|x|\lt 1$): $$\ln\dfrac{1+x}{1-x}=2\left(x+\dfrac{x^{3}}{3}+\dfrac{x^{5}}{5}+\dfrac{x^{7}}{7}+\dots\right) $$
  5. Natural logarithm of $x$ (for $x\gt 0$): $$\ln x=2\left[\dfrac{x-1}{x+1}+\dfrac{1}{3}\left(\dfrac{x-1}{x+1}\right)^{3}+\dfrac{1}{5}\left(\dfrac{x-1}{x+1}\right)^{5}+\dots\right] $$
  6. Cosine function: $$\cos x=1-\dfrac{x^{2}}{2!}+\dfrac{x^{4}}{4!}-\dfrac{x^{6}}{6!}+\dots+\dfrac{(-1)^{n}x^{2n}}{(2n)!}\pm\dots $$
  7. Sine function: $$ \sin x = x - \dfrac{x^{3}}{3!} + \dfrac{x^{5}}{5!} - \dfrac{x^{7}}{7!} + \dots + \dfrac{(-1)^{n}x^{2n+1}}{(2n+1)!} \pm \dots $$
  8. Tangent function ($|x| \lt \frac{\pi}{2}$): $$ \tan x = x + \dfrac{x^{3}}{3} + \dfrac{2x^{5}}{15} + \dfrac{17x^{7}}{315} + \dfrac{62x^{9}}{2835} + \dots $$
  9. Cotangent function ($|x| \lt \pi$): $$ \cot x = \dfrac{1}{x} - \left(\dfrac{x}{3} + \dfrac{x^{3}}{45} + \dfrac{2x^{5}}{945} + \dfrac{2x^{7}}{4725} + \dots \right) $$
  10. Arcsine function ($|x| \lt 1$): $$ \arcsin x = x + \dfrac{x^{3}}{2 \cdot 3} + \dfrac{1 \cdot 3x^{5}}{2 \cdot 4 \cdot 5} + \dots + \dfrac{1 \cdot 3 \cdot 5 \dots (2n-1)x^{2n+1}}{2 \cdot 4 \cdot 6 \dots (2n)(2n+1)} + \dots $$
  11. Arccosine function ($|x| \lt 1$): $$ \arccos x = \dfrac{\pi}{2} - \left(x + \dfrac{x^{3}}{2 \cdot 3} + \dfrac{1 \cdot 3x^{5}}{2 \cdot 4 \cdot 5} + \dots + \dfrac{1 \cdot 3 \cdot 5 \dots (2n-1)x^{2n+1}}{2 \cdot 4 \cdot 6 \dots (2n)(2n+1)} + \dots \right) $$
  12. Arctangent function ($|x| \le 1$): $$ \arctan x = x - \dfrac{x^{3}}{3} + \dfrac{x^{5}}{5} - \dfrac{x^{7}}{7} + \dots + \dfrac{(-1)^{n}x^{2n+1}}{2n+1} \pm \dots $$
  13. Hyperbolic cosine: $$ \cosh x = 1 + \dfrac{x^{2}}{2!} + \dfrac{x^{4}}{4!} + \dfrac{x^{6}}{6!} + \dots + \dfrac{x^{2n}}{(2n)!} + \dots $$
  14. Hyperbolic sine: $$ \sinh x = x + \dfrac{x^{3}}{3!} + \dfrac{x^{5}}{5!} + \dfrac{x^{7}}{7!} + \dots + \dfrac{x^{2n+1}}{(2n+1)!} + \dots $$

Binomial Series

Whole numbers: $n, m$
Real number: $x$
Combinations: ${}^{n}C_{m}$

  1. Binomial expansion: $$(1+x)^{n}=1+{}^{n}C_{1}x+{}^{n}C_{2}x^{2}+\dots+{}^{n}C_{m}x^{m}+\dots+x^{n} $$
  2. Combination formula (for $|x|\lt 1$): $${}^{n}C_{m}=\dfrac{n(n-1)\dots[n-(m-1)]}{m!} $$
  3. Geometric series expansion for $\frac{1}{1+x}$ ($|x|\lt 1$): $$\dfrac{1}{1+x}=1-x+x^{2}-x^{3}+\dots $$
  4. Geometric series expansion for $\frac{1}{1-x}$ ($|x|\lt 1$): $$\dfrac{1}{1-x}=1+x+x^{2}+x^{3}+\dots $$
  5. Square root expansion ($|x|\le 1$): $$\sqrt{1+x}=1+\dfrac{x}{2}-\dfrac{x^{2}}{2\cdot4}+\dfrac{1\cdot3x^{3}}{2\cdot4\cdot6}-\dfrac{1\cdot3\cdot5x^{4}}{2\cdot4\cdot6\cdot8}+\dots $$
  6. Cube root expansion ($|x|\le 1$): $$\sqrt[3]{1+x}=1+\dfrac{x}{3}-\dfrac{1\cdot2x^{2}}{3\cdot6}+\dfrac{1\cdot2\cdot5x^{3}}{3\cdot6\cdot9}-\dfrac{1\cdot2\cdot5\cdot8x^{4}}{3\cdot6\cdot9\cdot12}+\dots $$

11.13 Fourier Series

Integrable function: $f(x)$
Fourier coefficients: $a_{0}, a_{n}, b_{n}$
Whole number: $n$

  1. Fourier Series expansion: $$ f(x) = \dfrac{a_{0}}{2} + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} (a_{n} \cos nx + b_{n} \sin nx) $$
  2. Fourier coefficient $a_{n}$: $$ a_{n} = \dfrac{1}{\pi} \int_{-\pi}^{\pi} f(x) \cos nx \, dx $$
  3. Fourier coefficient $b_{n}$: $$ b_{n} = \dfrac{1}{\pi} \int_{-\pi}^{\pi} f(x) \sin nx \, dx $$
