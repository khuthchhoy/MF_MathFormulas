Quadratic Equation $ax^2+bx+c=0$

The solutions: $x=\dfrac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2-4ac}}{2a}$ If $a$, $b$, $c$ are real and if $D=b^2-4ac$ is the discriminant, then the rootare (1) real and unequal if $D > 0$ (2) real and equal if $D = 0$ (3) complex conjugate if $D < 0$ If $x_1$, $x_2$ are the roots, then $x_1+x_2=-\dfrac{b}{a}$ and $x_1x_2=\dfrac{c}{a}$. \section{Cubic Equation: $x^3+a_1x^2+a_2x+a_3=0$} Let $$\begin{aligned} &Q=\dfrac{3a_2-a_1^2}{9}\\ &R=\dfrac{9a_1a_2-27a_3-2a_1^3}{54}\\ &S=\sqrt[3]{R+\sqrt{Q^3+R^2}} \\ &T=\sqrt[3]{R-\sqrt{Q^3+R^2}} \end{aligned}$$ where $ST=-Q$. Solutions: $$\begin{cases}x_1=S+T-\dfrac{1}{3}a_1\\ x_2=-\dfrac{1}{2}(S+T)-\dfrac{1}{3}a_1+\dfrac{1}{2}i\sqrt{3}(S-T)\\ x_3=-\dfrac{1}{2}(S+T)-\dfrac{1}{3}a_1-\dfrac{1}{2}i\sqrt{3}(S-T)\end{cases}$$ If $a_1, a_2, a_3$ are real and if $D=Q^3+R^2$ is the discriminant, then (1) one root is real and two are complex conjugate if $D > 0$ (2) all roots are real and at least two are equal if $D = 0$ (3) all roots are real and unequal if $D < 0$ If $D < 0$, computation is simplified by using of trigonometry.
