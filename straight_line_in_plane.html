Point coordinates: $X,Y,x,x_0,x_1,y_0,y_1,a_1,a_2,...$
Real numbers: $k,a,b,p,t,A,B,C,A_1,A_2,...$
Angles: $\alpha,\beta$
Angle between two lines: $\phi$
Normal vector: $\vec{n}$
Position vectors: $\vec{r}, \vec{a},\vec{b}$

  1. General Equation of a Straight Line $$Ax + By + C = 0$$
  2. Normal Vector to a Staight Line
    The vector $\vec{n}(A,B)$ is normal to the line $Ax+By+C=0$.
  3. Explicit Equation of a Straight Line (Slope-Intercept Form) $$y = kx + b$$ The gradient of the line is $k=\tan \alpha$.
  4. Gradient of a Line $$k = \tan \alpha = \dfrac{y_2-y_1}{x_2-x_1}$$
  5. Equation of a Line Given a Point and the Gradient $$y=y_0+k\big(x-x_0\big),$$ where $k$ is the gradient, $P\Big( x_0,y_0\Big) is a point one the line.
  6. Equation of a Line That Passes Through Two Points $$\begin{aligned} &\frac{y-y_1}{y_2-y_1}=\frac{x-x_1}{x_2-x_1}\\ \\ &\text{or} \\ &\begin{vmatrix}x & y & 1\\ x_1& y_1 & 1 \\ x_2 & y_2&1 \end{vmatrix}=0 \end{aligned}$$
  7. Intercept Form $$\dfrac{x}{a}+\dfrac{y}{b}=1$$
  8. Normal Form $$x\cos \beta + y\sin\beta - p =0$$
  9. Point Direction Form $$\dfrac{x-x_1}{X}=\dfrac{y-y_1}{Y}$$ where $(X,Y)$ is the direction of the line and $P_1\left(x_1,y_1\right)$ lies on the line.
  10. Vertical Line $x=a$.
  11. Horizontal Line $y=b$.
  12. Vector Equation of a Straight Line $$\vec{r}=\vec{a}+t\vec{b},$$ where
    $O$ is the origin of the coordinates,
    $X$ is any variable point on the line,
    $\vec{a}$ is the position vector of a known point $A$ on the line,
    $\vec{b}$ is a known vector of direction, parallel to the line,
    $t$ is a parameter,
    $\vec{r}=\overrightarrow{OX}$ is the position vector of any point $X$ on the line.
  13. Straight Line in Parametric Form $$\begin{cases} x=a_1+tb_1\\ y=a_2+tb_2 \end{cases},$$ where
    $(x,y)$ are the coordinates of any unknown point on the line,
    $\left(a_1,a_2\right)$ are the coordinates of a known point one the line,
    $\left(b_1,b_2\right)$ are the coordinates of a vector parallel to the line,
    $t$ is a parameter.
  14. Distance From a Point To a Line
    The distance from the point $P(a,b)$ to the line $Ax+By+C=0$ is $$d=\dfrac{\Big|Aa+Bb+C\Big|}{\sqrt{A^2+B^2}}$$
  15. Parallel Lines
    Two lines $y=k_1x+b_1$ and $y=k_2x+b_2$ are parallel if $$k=k_1$$ Two lines $A_1x+B_1y+C_1=0$ and $A_2x+By_2+C_2=0$ are parallel if $$\dfrac{A_1}{A_2}=\dfrac{B_1}{B_2}$$
  16. Perpendicular Line
    Two lines $y=k_1x+b_1$ and $y=k_2x+b_2$ are perpendicular if $k_2=-\dfrac{1}{k_1}$ or, equivalently, $k_1k_2=-1$.
    Two lines $A_1x+B_1y+C_1=0$ and $A_2x+By_2+C_2=0$ are perpendicular if $$A_1A_2+B_1B_2=0$$
  17. Angle Between Two Lines $$\begin{aligned} &\tan \varphi = \frac{k_2-k_1}{1+k_1k_2}, \\\\ &\cos \varphi = \frac{A_1A_2 + B_1B_2}{\sqrt{A_1^2+B_1^2}\cdot\sqrt{A_2^2+B_2^2}} \end{aligned}$$
  18. Intersection of Two Lines
    If two lines $A_1x+B_1y+C_1=0$ and $A_2x+B_2y+C_2=0$ intersect, the intersection point has coordinates $$x_0=\dfrac{-C_1B_2+C_2B_1}{A_1B_2 -A_2B_1},\,y_0=\dfrac{-A_1C_2+A_2C_1}{A_1B_2-A_2B_1}$$
