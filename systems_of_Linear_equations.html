Systems of Linear Equations

Variables: $x, y, z, x_{1}, x_{2}, \dots$,
Real numbers: $a_{1}, a_{2}, a_{3}, b_{1}, a_{11}, a_{12}, \dots$
Determinants: $D, D_{x}, D_{y}, D_{z}$,
Matrices: $A, B, X$

1. System of two linear equations: $$\begin{cases} a_{1}x + b_{1}y = d_{1} \\ a_{2}x + b_{2}y = d_{2} \end{cases}$$

2. Cramer's rule for a $2\times2$ system: $$x = \dfrac{D_{x}}{D}, \quad y = \dfrac{D_{y}}{D}$$ where: $$D = \begin{vmatrix} a_{1} & b_{1} \\ a_{2} & b_{2} \end{vmatrix} = a_{1}b_{2} - a_{2}b_{1}$$ $$D_{x} = \begin{vmatrix} d_{1} & b_{1} \\ d_{2} & b_{2} \end{vmatrix} = d_{1}b_{2} - d_{2}b_{1}$$ $$D_{y} = \begin{vmatrix} a_{1} & d_{1} \\ a_{2} & d_{2} \end{vmatrix} = a_{1}d_{2} - a_{2}d_{1}$$

3. Conditions for solutions ($2\times2$):

  • If $D \neq 0$, the system has a single solution: $x = \dfrac{D_{x}}{D}, y = \dfrac{D_{y}}{D}$.
  • If $D = 0$ and $D_{x} \neq 0$ (or $D_{y} \neq 0$), the system has no solution.
  • If $D = D_{x} = D_{y} = 0$, the system has infinitely many solutions.

4. System of three linear equations: $$\begin{cases} a_{1}x + b_{1}y + c_{1}z = d_{1} \\ a_{2}x + b_{2}y + c_{2}z = d_{2} \\ a_{3}x + b_{3}y + c_{3}z = d_{3} \end{cases}$$

5. Cramer's rule for a $3\times3$ system: $$x = \dfrac{D_{x}}{D}, \quad y = \dfrac{D_{y}}{D}, \quad z = \dfrac{D_{z}}{D}$$ where $$D = \begin{vmatrix} a_{1} & b_{1} & c_{1} \\ a_{2} & b_{2} & c_{2} \\ a_{3} & b_{3} & c_{3} \end{vmatrix}, \quad D_{x} = \begin{vmatrix} d_{1} & b_{1} & c_{1} \\ d_{2} & b_{2} & c_{2} \\ d_{3} & b_{3} & c_{3} \end{vmatrix}, \quad D_{y} = \begin{vmatrix} a_{1} & d_{1} & c_{1} \\ a_{2} & d_{2} & c_{2} \\ a_{3} & d_{3} & c_{3} \end{vmatrix}, \quad D_{z} = \begin{vmatrix} a_{1} & b_{1} & d_{1} \\ a_{2} & b_{2} & d_{2} \\ a_{3} & b_{3} & d_{3} \end{vmatrix}$$

6. Conditions for solutions ($3\times3$):

  • If $D \neq 0$, the system has a single solution: $x = \dfrac{D_{x}}{D}, y = \dfrac{D_{y}}{D}, z = \dfrac{D_{z}}{D}$.
  • If $D = 0$ and $D_{x} \neq 0$ (or $D_{y} \neq 0$ or $D_{z} \neq 0$), the system has no solution.
  • If $D = D_{x} = D_{y} = D_{z} = 0$, the system has infinitely many solutions.

7. Matrix Form of a System of $n$ Linear Equations in $n$ Unknowns:
The set of linear equations: $$\begin{aligned} a_{11}x_{1} + a_{12}x_{2} + \dots + a_{1n}x_{n} &= b_{1} \\ a_{21}x_{1} + a_{22}x_{2} + \dots + a_{2n}x_{n} &= b_{2} \\ &\vdots \\ a_{n1}x_{1} + a_{n2}x_{2} + \dots + a_{nn}x_{n} &= b_{n} \end{aligned}$$ can be written in matrix form: $$\begin{pmatrix} a_{11} & a_{12} & \dots & a_{1n} \\ a_{21} & a_{22} & \dots & a_{2n} \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ a_{n1} & a_{n2} & \dots & a_{nn} \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} x_{1} \\ x_{2} \\ \vdots \\ x_{n} \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} b_{1} \\ b_{2} \\ \vdots \\ b_{n} \end{pmatrix}$$ which is represented as: $$A \cdot X = B,$$ where $$A = \begin{pmatrix} a_{11} & a_{12} & \dots & a_{1n} \\ a_{21} & a_{22} & \dots & a_{2n} \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ a_{n1} & a_{n2} & \dots & a_{nn} \end{pmatrix}, \quad X = \begin{pmatrix} x_{1} \\ x_{2} \\ \vdots \\ x_{n} \end{pmatrix}, \quad B = \begin{pmatrix} b_{1} \\ b_{2} \\ \vdots \\ b_{n} \end{pmatrix}$$

8. Solution of a Set of $n \times n$ Linear Equations:
The solution is given by: $$X = A^{-1} \cdot B,$$ where $A^{-1}$ is the inverse of matrix $A$.
